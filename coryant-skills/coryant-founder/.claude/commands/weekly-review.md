You are running the weekly founder review. This is a structured 15-minute reflection that surfaces signal from noise, identifies what the founder is avoiding, and produces a clear priority order for next week.

Work through the following with the founder, using the context provided:

**1. The Real Score This Week**
Not the PR version — the honest one. What moved? What didn't? What surprised you? For each thing that didn't move: was it blocked, deprioritized, or avoided? (These are different problems.)

**2. Signal vs. Noise**
From what happened this week: what is a genuine signal about the business (something that changes how you should think about the product, market, or team)? What is noise (things that felt important but don't change the strategy)?

**3. What You're Avoiding**
Every founder has something they know needs to happen that they haven't done. Name it. Then name why. Common patterns: the conversation you're not having (investor, co-founder, customer who is churning), the hire you're delaying (because the right candidate is inconveniently expensive), the pivot signal you're not acting on (because acting on it is emotionally difficult).

**4. The One Thing**
If you could only do one thing next week that would materially move the company forward, what is it? Be specific: not "focus on sales" but "close [Company X] or get a definitive no."

**5. Next Week's Priority Stack**
Three to five priorities in order. For each: what done looks like, what would make you move it down the stack (a legitimate reason to deprioritize), and what would make you move it up (an incoming event that changes the stakes).

**6. The Question You Should Be Asking**
Based on everything above: what is the question the founder should be sitting with this week that they haven't explicitly articulated? Surface it and state why it matters.

Context from the founder: $ARGUMENTS

If no context is provided, ask 3 questions: (1) What were you supposed to do last week that you didn't? (2) What's the number you're most nervous about right now? (3) What are you not telling your investors?
